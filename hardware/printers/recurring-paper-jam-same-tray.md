# Recurring Paper Jam, Same Tray

- **Category:** Hardware
- **OSI Layer:** N/A
- **Date documented:** 2026-05-25
- **Status:** Resolved

## Symptoms
- Jams repeatedly in the same tray/path, even after clearing

## Environment
- Paper type/weight, tray number, printer model

## Diagnostic Steps
1. Inspect that tray's feed rollers for wear/glaze — shiny smooth patches mean lost grip.
2. Check paper is within the fill line and not curled/humid.
3. Run the printer's built-in jam diagnostic, if it has one.
4. Check that tray's feed sensor isn't dirty or obstructed.

## Root Cause
Worn feed rollers losing grip — the strongest signal is that it's consistently the same tray rather than random.

## Solution
1. Clean rollers with a lint-free cloth and isopropyl alcohol first — often fixes "glazed" rollers.
2. Replace the paper with a fresh ream; humidity-affected paper jams disproportionately.
3. If cleaning doesn't help, replace that tray's feed/pickup roller assembly.

## Verification
- 50+ pages print from that tray with no jam.

## Prevention
- Replace feed rollers on the manufacturer's recommended page-count interval, not just on failure.
